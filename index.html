One morning, I awoke with a restless flutter in my chest. The cold morning light filtered through my curtains as I stretched, every muscle tingling with unease. After a brisk shower, I toweled off and felt my stomach rumble. Hunger drove me into the bright streets, each step echoing in the quiet dawn.

Around the corner, a rustic diner beckoned—its windows fogged with steam, the scent of freshly baked bread and sizzling bacon drifting out. I pressed my hand to the glass, anticipation fluttering in my chest.

Just as I reached for the door handle, two masked figures emerged from the shadows behind me. My pulse spiked. A brutal crack at the base of my skull sent stars dancing across my vision. Before I could cry out, rough hands bound my wrists and ankles with thick rope.

Panic seized me as they dragged me to a narrow alley. Cold stone pressed against my face, the air heavy with damp decay. They shoved me headfirst into the back of a matte-black van, its interior smelling of motor oil and fear. The doors clanged shut.

The van sped off, tires screeching around corners I couldn’t identify. Each lurch of the vehicle sent waves of nausea rolling through me, and my vision blurred. Darkness claimed me once more.

When I stirred again, I lay sprawled on a cold concrete floor. My head throbbed, and the van’s doors yawned open to reveal an abandoned warehouse swallowed by dense forest. Overhead, a single bulb swayed like a pendulum, casting frantic shadows on faded graffiti.

Two figures yanked me to my feet, their silhouettes stark against the green glow of countless glass cylinders arranged on metal racks. Each tube pulsed with luminescent fluid, making the hybrids inside drift like ghosts in water—winged women, fish-tailed men, horned children, all trapped between worlds.

My breathing caught. The air was thick with antiseptic and something sharp, chemical. My skin crawled.

Before I could process, they shoved me toward an empty cylinder. Its cold glass door slammed shut behind me. A hiss above signaled release—glowing green liquid poured from a nozzle, soaking my clothes and searing my flesh. I screamed until I could scream no more.

When consciousness returned, the ropes were gone—but everything felt off. My senses roared to life: each drip of fluid echoed like thunder, every breath a symphony of pain. A strange tug at my back made me shiver.

Heart hammering, I peered through the glass wall. Two soft, triangular ears—white and velvety—crowned my head. A long, fluffy tail swished behind me, brushing the cold cylinder floor. My reflection stared back, eyes wide with horror.

Time slid by in a blur of panic. Finally, the cylinder door clanged open. I stumbled out, disoriented and trembling.

I swallowed hard, dread coiling in my gut. With no other choice, I nodded.

They handed me a delicate maid’s dress—soft lace, ribbons the color of cherry blossoms—and a jingling bell collar. My cheeks flamed hotter than any pain I’d felt. I dressed, each fold of fabric a reminder of my humiliation.

Broom in hand, I spent dawn to dusk polishing floors, scrubbing counters, my tail brushing each surface in rhythmic sweeps. Each clang of the metal bucket was a drumbeat of my new reality.

Night fell, and they locked me in a tiny cage suspended from the rafters—a grotesque swing in the silent warehouse. I pressed my back against the cold metal, tail curled tightly around my legs, praying for rescue.

On the third night, they dragged me from my cage, gagged and bound for the kitchen. My heart sank at the sight of a massive cauldron bubbling with scalding oil—steam rising in angry clouds.

Captor 1: “Time to see how our little kitty fares,”

He hoisted me toward the cauldron’s lip. I braced for agony—

Then pandemonium. The oil splashed over me, scorching my back, and I let out a muffled scream through my gag as the captors recoiled in shock.

A door burst open. A dark-haired woman stormed in, gun in hand. Two precise shots later, the captors crumpled. She raced to me, scissors flashing as she cut my ropes and yanked out the gag.
